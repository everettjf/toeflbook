## 1 · Why Interview is hard (and why AI helps)

The new TOEFL 2026 Interview task asks 4 questions, each 45 sec, **with no preparation time**. ETS samples cluster into 3 styles:

- **Opinion/preference:** "Do you prefer studying alone or in a group? Why?"
- **Memory/experience:** "Describe a place you visited that surprised you."
- **Lifestyle/routine:** "How do you usually relax after a stressful week?"

Standard prep mistake: students "prepare" 50 canned topics. The new AI scorer **detects canned answers** and penalizes them. What works instead:

1. **Structural fluency** — Idea (0–15s) → Reason (15–30s) → Tie-in (30–45s). Apply *any* topic, on the fly.
2. **Volume of spontaneous reps** — only a conversational AI (**R-AI-1 PrepEx Petra**) can give you 50+ unique reps in 2 weeks.
3. **Delivery > complexity** — clarity, pace, intelligibility beat vocabulary fireworks.

## 6 · Common pitfalls

1. **The new test penalizes canned answers.** Don't memorize 50 sample answers. Memorize the **structure** (15-15-15 sec).
2. **AI Petra is not ETS — but the gap doesn't matter.** What you're training is fluency volume. 50 Petra reps > 5 perfect ETS-scored attempts.
3. **Time mis-management is the #1 fail.** Practice with a visible timer. Stop talking the second the 45 sec mark hits — train the reflex.
4. **Hesitations are fine; restarts are not.** "Um, well, I think..." is normal speech. "Wait, let me start over" tanks the score. Practice **committing** to whatever answer falls out of your mouth.
5. **Don't drift back to Independent Speaking templates.** Those are extinct in the new format. The AI scorer flags template-y openings ("In my opinion, the most important factor...").
