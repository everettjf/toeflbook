---

## 1 · Why this chapter exists

The new TOEFL 2026 Speaking Section is **7 Listen & Repeat items + 4 Interview items**. There is **no preparation time** for either. The Listen & Repeat task tests:

- **Auditory memory** (can you hold 8–15 words in working memory for ~3 sec)
- **Pronunciation fidelity** (consonant clusters, final consonants, linked sounds)
- **Prosody match** (stress, rhythm, intonation)
- **Speed of decoding** (no time to "think about" what you heard)

These are **exactly** what shadowing trains. Hamada (2016) and the 2025 Tandfonline systematic review converge: 4–6 weeks of daily shadowing → measurable gains on all four. This chapter doubles down.

## 6 · Common pitfalls in this chapter

1. **Recording is not optional.** You can't hear your own pronunciation errors live — only on playback. If you skip recording, the chapter doesn't work.
2. **3-pass protocol matters.** "Blind shadow only" gives ~50% of the benefit. The transcript pass exposes what you missed; the record-compare pass closes the loop.
3. **Don't memorize sentences.** L&R items are random. The skill is *generalized auditory memory*, not "I happen to know this sentence."
4. **Match stress, not just words.** AI scorers (and ETS) weigh prosody. Saying every syllable correctly but flat = lower score than 1 word wrong + good stress.
5. **Minimal-pair work** if pronunciation is your bottleneck: free resource is **Cambridge Sounds Pronunciation Drills** (search YouTube) — focus on /θ/ /ð/ /r/ /l/ /v/ /w/.
6. **Don't sacrifice Reading/Listening this chapter.** The plan keeps them at MAIN slots for a reason — gains evaporate fast.
