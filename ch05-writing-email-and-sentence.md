## 1 · Why Email is its own chapter

In the new TOEFL 2026 Writing section, Email is **its own scored task** (not a warm-up). It tests:

- **Register awareness** (professor vs. peer vs. admin staff)
- **Functional clarity** (greeting → context → request → close)
- **Polite directness** — neither blunt nor over-padded
- **Grammar and word choice under time pressure** (10 min hard ceiling)

Build a Sentence tests pure **syntactic competence** — word-order under time pressure. Both reward **clean, simple structures** over showy vocabulary.

## 6 · Common pitfalls

1. **Email is short — don't pad.** 100 words means 100 words. AI scorers (and ETS) penalize padding ("I would like to take this opportunity to..."). Be functional.
2. **Greeting + close take 15% of your word count.** Budget for it. Don't waste space on flourishes.
3. **Build a Sentence is grammar, not style.** If you miss items, drill **word order** (subject-verb-object inversions, adverb placement, comparatives) — don't add vocabulary.
4. **AI scoring on Email is mediocre** — heavily weight your own re-read. ChatGPT/Claude are better for "is this natural?" than for numeric scores.
5. **Don't let L&R rot.** It needed 14 days of intensity to build; it can lose 30% in 14 days of neglect. Keep the L&R micro-drill alive in Ch.5+.
