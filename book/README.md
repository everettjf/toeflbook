## 0 · Why this book exists

On **2026-01-21**, ETS launched a completely redesigned **Adaptive TOEFL iBT**. The exam you'll sit in September is this new version — not an updated old TOEFL but a different game with different rules:

**Total length**
- Old: ~120 min
- **New: 90 min**

**Reading / Listening**
- Old: Fixed items
- **New: Adaptive — Module 1 difficulty determines Module 2**

**Speaking tasks**
- Old: 4 tasks (Independent + Integrated)
- **New: 2 tasks — Listen & Repeat (7 items) + Interview (4 items)**

**Speaking prep time**
- Old: Yes
- **New: None**

**Writing tasks**
- Old: Integrated + Academic Discussion
- **New: Build a Sentence + Email + Academic Discussion**

**Score**
- Old: 0–120
- **New: 1–6 CEFR band** (0–120 also reported for 2 years)

**Score delivery**
- Old: 6–10 days
- **New: 72 hours**

**Templated answers**
- Old: Workable
- **New: AI detects them → penalty**

**Three rules that run through every chapter:**

1. **Early items are not equal.** The adaptive engine punishes Module 1 mistakes more heavily. All "accuracy training" in this book targets the early items.
2. **Real input beats drill.** ETS's own research (Liu, 2014; see `resources.md` § 7, R-REF-1) found coaching attendance has near-zero correlation with score. **Total real-English exposure** is the main driver. Every week includes real material (academic podcasts, TED, Coursera).
3. **No templates.** The new AI scorer penalizes templated language. This book trains **structural thinking** (Email 4-part frame, Discussion PEEL reasoning) — not phrases to memorize.

## 1 · Methodology (evidence-based)

### 1.1 Shadowing
Originated in 1990s Japanese/Korean EFL pedagogy. Meta-analyses (Hamada 2016; Tandfonline 2025 systematic review) show:
- **10–15 min/day × 4–6 weeks** → measurable gains in listening comprehension, fluency, pronunciation
- **Directly maps** to Listen & Repeat (the new 7/11 Speaking items)
- This book reserves **15 min/day for shadowing**. See `resources.md` § 4 for sources and the 3-pass protocol.

### 1.2 Spaced repetition (Anki)
- Vocabulary is the floor; everything else stacks on it.
- Decks: **4000 Essential English Words** (general) + **Barron's Essential Words for the TOEFL** (academic).
- 15 min/day = 20 new + ~60 reviews. See `resources.md` § 3.

### 1.3 Adaptive test strategy
- **Module 1 accuracy > speed.** Push hard early to unlock harder Module 2 (= higher ceiling).
- **Error log with attribution.** For every wrong answer, tag it: *knowledge gap / careless / time pressure*. Careless errors hurt most.
- **No templates.** But do learn **frames**: Email (greeting → context → ask → close), Discussion (Point → Evidence → Explain → Link).

### 1.4 AI tool stack
Picked one per role; see `resources.md` § 2 for full catalog.

- **Official mock + scoring:** ETS TestReady — only platform with the actual scoring engine
- **Conversational speaking coach:** PrepEx (Petra AI) — fixes the "no prep time" problem; live dialogue
- **Cross-section AI scoring:** FluentPrep AI — ETS-rubric-aligned, 900+ items
- **Vocabulary:** Anki + shared decks — proven spaced-repetition algorithm
- **Writing polish:** ChatGPT / Claude — grammar/idea feedback only; **never trust their scores**
- **Shadowing source:** TED-Ed / 60-Second Science — transcript-bundled, ~C1 register

## 2 · The 90-Minute Daily Template (the iron rule)

### 2.1 Daily time blocks

```
00:00–00:15  ▌ Anki vocabulary       (20 new + ~60 reviews)         ← every day
00:15–00:30  ▌ Shadowing             (Listening + Speaking combined) ← every day
00:30–01:00  ▌ Day's MAIN skill      (30 min — rotates per § 2.2)
01:00–01:20  ▌ Day's SECONDARY skill (20 min — rotates per § 2.2)
01:20–01:30  ▌ Error log             (3 lines: what / why / how to avoid next time)
```

> Note: **Anki already covers vocabulary (foundation for all 4 skills).** **Shadowing already trains Listening + Speaking simultaneously.** So the "Main + Secondary" 30+20-min blocks are **additional** focused work on top of those two foundations.

### 2.2 Weekly skill rotation (iron rule: all four skills touched every week)

- **Monday** · MAIN = Reading (30 min) · SECONDARY = Writing (20 min) · Touches: R, W, L+S (shadowing)
- **Tuesday** · MAIN = Listening (30 min) · SECONDARY = Speaking (20 min) · Touches: L, S, L+S
- **Wednesday** · MAIN = Writing (30 min) · SECONDARY = Reading (20 min) · Touches: W, R, L+S
- **Thursday** · MAIN = Speaking (30 min) · SECONDARY = Listening (20 min) · Touches: S, L, L+S
- **Friday** · MAIN = Reading (30 min) · SECONDARY = Speaking (20 min) · Touches: R, S, L+S
- **Saturday** · MAIN = Listening + Speaking combined (30 min) · SECONDARY = Writing (20 min) · Touches: L, S, W
- **Sunday** · MAIN = Writing — Email or Discussion (30 min) · SECONDARY = Short mock + weekly review (20 min) · Touches: W + full skill recap

**Weekly focused touches (MAIN + SECONDARY + combined):** R×3, L×3, W×4, S×4 (combined L+S counted once for each) ⇒ **every skill ≥ 3 focused sessions per week**.

### 2.3 Sunday extra: review + short mock
Sundays use 30 min for a short mock-style segment (~10 reading items OR 8 listening items OR 1 writing task), pulled from any free source in `resources.md` § 5 (Magoosh, TestSucceed, TestReady free items). Remaining time on error-log review. This is where weekly practice **converts into score**.

### 2.4 The floor rules
- **Anki + Shadowing never get skipped.** They are the floor. Break either for 3 consecutive days and the whole system collapses.
- **The error log matters more than item count.** Practice without review = zero.

## 3 · 8 Chapters + 1 Taper — Roadmap

> ⚠️ **Important:** Each chapter's "theme" is an **emphasis on top of** the weekly rotation — not "this chapter only practices one skill."
> The § 2.2 rotation is always in effect. The chapter theme just shifts MAIN sessions toward the focus skill.
> One-time tool setup + the 4-skill baseline mock are folded into **Ch.1, Day 1–2** (see that chapter's § 0).

**Ch.1 — 6/1 to 6/14 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Listening + high-frequency vocabulary (Day 1–2: setup + 4-skill baseline)
- Deliverable: Weak-skill profile + tools live + Listening baseline (free TestReady/Magoosh items)

**Ch.2 — 6/15 to 6/28 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Reading + academic vocabulary
- Deliverable: Reading Module 1 ≥ 85% accuracy

**Ch.3 — 6/29 to 7/12 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Speaking — Listen & Repeat focus
- Deliverable: 7-item exact-repeat rate ≥ 80%

**Ch.4 — 7/13 to 7/26 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Writing — Email + Build a Sentence
- Deliverable: Email 100 words in 10 min

**Ch.5 — 7/27 to 8/9 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Speaking — Interview + AI dialogue
- Deliverable: Structured 45-sec Interview answer

**Ch.6 — 8/10 to 8/23 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Writing — Academic Discussion
- Deliverable: 200-word Discussion in 10 min

**Ch.7 — 8/24 to 9/6 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Adaptive realism — Module 1 mastery
- Deliverable: 4-skill Module 1 accuracy on target

**Ch.8 — 9/7 to 9/20 (14 days)**
- Emphasis: Full mocks ×4 + error attribution
- Deliverable: Stable mock score ≥ 100

**Final Taper (Ch.9) — 9/21 to 9/24 (4 days)**
- Emphasis: Pre-exam tune-up + state management (taper week compressed)
- Deliverable: Peak on exam morning

**Pre-exam rest — 9/25 Fri** (off — Anki maintenance only, no new content)
**Exam day — 9/26 Sat 08:00**

**What "emphasis" means in practice:** in Ch.1 (Listening focus week), the Sunday "MAIN = Writing" slot temporarily becomes a "Listening micro-mock," and Shadowing source switches to lecture-style audio. Across the week, Listening focused touches rise while **Reading, Writing, Speaking each still get ≥ 3 focused sessions** + daily Shadowing + daily Anki. Each chapter's intro restates its modified weekly schedule.

## 6 · How to use this book

1. **Each morning:** open the current chapter file, tick today's checkboxes
2. **Each evening:** add to the error log (3 lines is fine — *what / why / how to avoid*)
3. **Every other Saturday:** complete the chapter's **bi-week review card** at the bottom of the chapter file
4. **If you miss a target:** push the lagging task into next chapter's early days — don't force progress
5. **Anki and Shadowing are not optional.** Anything else can be halved on a bad day; these two never stop.
