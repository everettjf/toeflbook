# Ch.9 · Full Mocks ×4 + Error Attribution (9/7 – 9/20)

> **Days:** 14 (Day 1 = Mon 9/7, Day 14 = Sun 9/20)
> **Emphasis:** Whole-test execution under real conditions; turning practice into score.
> **Skill coverage:** Mocks are inherently all-4-skill. Anki + Shadowing floor.
> **Deliverables:** **4 full mocks** completed with score trend visible, stabilizing at **≥ 100** (target band) by Day 14 + thorough error-attribution log + exam-day routine rehearsed.

---

## 1 · The Ch.9 rhythm

This is the chapter where you stop "studying TOEFL" and start "executing TOEFL."

**Each mock cycle = 3 days:**
- **Mock Day:** 90 minutes, full simulation (single 90-min block, no phone, no music)
- **Attribution Day:** the entire 90 minutes is spent **on the mock's errors** — not new practice
- **Active recovery + targeted drill Day:** light Anki + Shadowing + 1 quick drill on the weakest skill the mock exposed

You do this 4 times (3 × 4 = 12 days) — with **Day 7 inserted as a deliberate light/taper day** between cycles 2 and 3 — leaving Day 14 for the Bi-week review + exam-day dress rehearsal.

### Mock source plan (all free — $0 total)

- **Mock 1** → **R-FREE-1** Magoosh free 2026 practice test (https://magoosh.com/toefl/toefl-practice-test/)
  - _Why this one here:_ Full sections, free signup, fresh content. Calibrate yourself before the higher-stakes mocks.
- **Mock 2** → **R-FREE-3** TestSucceed 2026 sample materials (https://testsucceed.com/materials/tests/toefl_new)
  - _Why this one here:_ Has examples for every new task type — broad coverage.
- **Mock 3** → **R-OFF-1** ETS TestReady free-tier full mock (https://www.ets.org/toefl/test-takers/ibt/prepare/toefl-testready.html)
  - _Why this one here:_ Official scoring engine. **This is the one where you must hit ≥ 100.** Use whatever full adaptive mock is included in your TestReady free tier.
- **Mock 4** → **R-OFF-2** ETS Sample Test Jan 2026 (https://www.ets.org/toefl/test-takers/ibt/prepare/sample-test-jan-2026-1.html) (retake)
  - _Why this one here:_ Most format-accurate free option. You did this in Ch.1 — 4 months later you've forgotten enough that the retake is honest. Compare scaled score to your Day 2–3 baseline = headline progress number.

> **Why mix sources?** Same-source repetition trains you on that source's quirks (item style, scoring tendency), not the test itself. Mixing forces generalization — closer to real-exam behavior.
>
> **The "I want one more official mock" trap.** If after Mock 4 you feel you need another full retired official test, the only true option is **R-OFF-5** TPO (https://www.ets.org/toefl/test-takers/ibt/prepare/practice-tests.html) ($45.95/volume). It is **optional and last-resort.** Don't buy it pre-emptively at Day 1 — only consider it if (a) you've actually finished all 4 mocks above and (b) Mock 4 was within 3 points of Mock 3 but below 100 and you want one more calibrated rep before exam day. Most people don't need it.

## 6 · Common pitfalls

1. **Don't mock every day.** Mocks are exhausting. The 3-day cycle (mock / attribute / recover) is research-backed and matches your physical capacity.
2. **Attribution > new items.** A wrong answer with no attribution is a guaranteed repeat. A wrong answer with attribution is half-fixed already.
3. **AI scoring on Speaking/Writing has a 5-point error bar.** Don't panic over a 3-point dip between mocks. Look at the trend across all 4.
4. **The light day (Day 7) is non-negotiable.** Cumulative fatigue tanks Mocks 3–4. Trust the taper.
5. **Mock 4 within 3 points of Mock 3 = ready.** Score volatility means the system isn't stable yet. If Mock 4 swings ±5 from Mock 3, examine: was it the mock content or your state?
6. **Don't compare to other test takers.** Your trend is the only data that matters.
