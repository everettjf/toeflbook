Standard rotation per `README` § 2.2 still applies. **Three changes:**

1. **Shadowing source** transitions to **dense academic text** — pick *Nature News* / *Science News* full articles (**R-REAL-3** — https://www.nature.com/news · https://www.sciencenews.org) or longer TED-Ed (**R-SHAD-1** https://ed.ted.com/lessons) covering science/history topics. ## 5 · Common pitfalls in this chapter

1. **"I understand the passage" ≠ "I'll get the questions right."** Comprehension and question-targeting are separate skills. Train them separately: read for gist → re-read for the specific question's evidence.
2. **Vocab-in-context is a free point** if your Anki habit holds. If you're missing those, you're behind on Anki, not on reading.
3. **Inference is not guessing.** Every inference answer is supported by a specific line in the text. Train yourself to **point to the line** before locking an answer.
4. **Complete the Words tests morphology + spelling, not meaning.** Practice prefixes/suffixes separately; many missed letters are predictable from word family (e.g., *-tion, -ment, un-, in-*).
5. **Don't skip Listening this chapter.** The Ch.1 gains evaporate in 2 weeks if you stop.
