## 5 · Common pitfalls in this chapter

1. **Don't replay a listening clip mid-attempt.** The test gives one pass — your practice must too.
2. **Notes aren't a transcript.** Capture structure (intro / 3 points / conclusion), not every word. Compare your notes to ETS's lecture outline (where provided) on Day 5 & 12.
3. **Anki fatigue is real.** If reviews stack above 100, **don't add new cards** until you're under 50 backlog. Better to skip new than drop reviews.
4. **Lecture vocab ≠ conversation vocab.** Lectures use formal academic register (e.g., *substantiate, predominantly, intricate*) — Barron's TOEFL deck is tuned for this.
5. **Speed isn't the problem early on.** Most missed listening items come from a single unknown word or distraction, not pace. Identify the cause before "trying to listen faster."
